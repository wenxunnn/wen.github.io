CONFIDENTIAL — CASE FILE #4471

Requesting Party: Margaret Hale, Family Attorney

To the Investigation Team,

My name is Margaret Hale. I've served as the Ashford family's attorney for almost twenty years, and I'm reaching out because I don't trust how quickly this case was closed.

Four nights ago, Theodore Ashford — founder of Ashford Specialty Catalysts and owner of Ashbourne Hall — was found dead in his study by his housekeeper, just before midnight. The official report calls it a tragic fall. The family wants it left at that.

I don't believe that's the whole story.

Theodore had asked me, privately, to review the company's books before the dinner that evening. He told me he'd found "irregularities" and that he intended to settle the matter quietly with the family before he announced who would take over the company. That conversation was supposed to happen after dinner. It never did.

I've gathered what I could before anyone thought to stop me: the medical examiner's summary, the police report, the estate's security log, and the company's own financial records. I'm locking each file behind a puzzle, because I'm afraid that if the wrong person reaches this archive first, the truth will quietly disappear — the way Theodore's "irregularities" so nearly did.

You are, by every account, the best detective team there is. Please — find out what really happened to Theodore that night.

— Margaret Hale

Case Closed

The Truth

Daniel Whitlock had been quietly siphoning company funds through inflated contractor invoices to cover a string of gambling debts. When Theodore ordered an outside audit of the books, the gap surfaced — and Theodore decided to settle it privately with the family rather than involve the police, at least until he'd heard Daniel out.

Daniel got to him first. The security log places him in the study at 3:55 PM — exactly when the examiner says Theodore died — despite his statement that he'd left hours earlier. He begged Theodore to keep it quiet; Theodore refused. In the confrontation that followed, Theodore fell and struck his head. Daniel staged the scene to look like an accident, then went back into the books to quietly patch the number he'd faked.

He didn't count on the accounting system keeping its own independent record — or on a detective team that knew how to read one.

Case #4471: Closed.
